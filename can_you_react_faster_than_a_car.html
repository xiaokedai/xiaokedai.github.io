Can You React Faster Than a Car?

A standalone classroom game about reaction time, judgment under uncertainty, and why driver-assistance systems matter. No Mobileye knowledge needed.
12 scenarios
Timed + untimed
Presenter-controlled

How the game works

  • You will see driving situations.
  • Some rounds are timed. Some rounds are untimed discussion rounds.
  • Choose A, B, or C on one shared screen.
  • After each answer, the game explains the safer choice and shows whether an accident happened in the simulation.

Best way to run it in class

  • Project this page on the screen.
  • Have everyone vote with fingers, paper cards, or by shouting A, B, or C.
  • You control the pace by clicking the choices and Next button.

Why this is engaging

The class feels how hard driving decisions become when time is short or information is missing. That makes ADAS feel real before you explain the technology.

This game shows one simple idea: common road decisions become hard when time is short, visibility is limited, or information is incomplete. That is exactly why driver-assistance systems matter.

Fast debrief questions

  • Which untimed scenario felt hardest, and why?
  • Did anyone choose differently because information was limited?
  • Which wrong choice looked reasonable at first?
  • Where would ADAS help most: warning, braking, or awareness?

Bridge back to your presentation

If these choices already feel stressful in a classroom, imagine them in real traffic at speed. Mobileye builds systems that help with exactly these moments: seeing danger earlier, processing it quickly, and assisting the driver before human error becomes a crash.
